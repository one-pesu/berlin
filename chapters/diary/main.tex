After a long day, Dr. Weißmüller made himself a cup of strong coffee and returned to his desk.
Before him lay the decrypted diary of Genosse Strahd.

A quick report from the cryptographers was clipped to the front page, its contents as unsettling as the diary itself.
The author, they concluded, had developed a unique script and a personal language.
Furthermore, they found a second, more complex layer of code embedded within the text.
The attached pages, however, were a direct, almost word-for-word translation of the primary narrative.

He took a sip of his coffee and settled in to read the words of a madman, a traitor, or a monster.
